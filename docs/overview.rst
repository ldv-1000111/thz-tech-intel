Technology Overview
===================

Teradar's proprietary **Terahertz Vision** platform operates in the 0.1–10 THz
electromagnetic band — positioned between microwave and infrared — and inherits
the best properties of both: the all-weather robustness of radar and the
sub-millimeter resolution of cameras and lidar.

The Sensing Problem
-------------------

Over 1.3 million road fatalities occur globally each year. An estimated 22% happen
under adverse weather or low-visibility conditions that outmatch traditional sensors.

Current ADAS sensor benchmarks require:

- Spatial resolution below 1° across 300 m+ ranges
- Multi-material detection (metal, plastic, textile, water)
- Reliable performance in all weather and lighting conditions
- Real-time classification of vulnerable road users

Cameras, radar, and lidar each address some of these requirements but none satisfies
all of them simultaneously in real-world edge cases.

How Terahertz Vision Works
--------------------------

THz waves (0.1–10 THz, wavelengths 3 mm–30 µm) unlock four unique capabilities:

**Sub-millimeter resolution**
  Short wavelengths deliver native angular resolution down to 0.1°, dramatically
  outperforming radar and matching or surpassing lidar in degraded weather.

**Material fingerprinting**
  THz waves stimulate vibrational modes in polymers, water vapor, and organic
  materials, yielding unique spectral signatures for advanced object classification.

**Non-ionizing safety**
  THz photons carry only millielectronvolts of energy — insufficient to disrupt
  DNA or chemical bonds — making them inherently safe for humans and vehicles.

**Immunity to glare**
  THz signals detect objects obscured by direct sunlight, headlights, or
  oncoming glare where cameras routinely fail.
