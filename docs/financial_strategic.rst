Financial & Strategic Intelligence
===================================

.. note::
   Last updated: May 2026. Data sourced from SEC filings, earnings calls, press releases,
   PitchBook, and company investor relations pages.

This page provides a deep-dive financial and strategic view of the seven ADAS sensing
companies tracked in this hub, covering funding history, revenue trajectory, OEM contract
status, technology roadmaps, and IP positioning — all focused on the automotive L2–L5 segment.

----

Funding & Financial Health
--------------------------

Teradar
~~~~~~~

Teradar emerged from stealth in November 2025 with a $150M Series B — the largest
single funding round in the THz sensing space. The round was led by VXI Capital with
participation from IBEX Investors, Capricorn Investment Group, The Engine Ventures (MIT),
and Lockheed Martin Ventures. Total funding stands at approximately $175M (PitchBook).
The company has 62 employees and is pre-revenue, with its first production win targeted
for 2028. The Lockheed Martin Ventures participation signals dual-use (automotive +
defense) investor confidence.

**Key signal:** $150M Series B at stealth exit is a strong vote of confidence.
The MIT Engine and Lockheed Martin combination is unusual — suggesting both deep-tech
credibility and near-term defense revenue as a bridge to automotive scale.

Arbe Robotics (Nasdaq: ARBE)
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

Arbe closed 2025 with approximately $52M in net cash following a $105M capital
infusion and an additional $18.5M direct offering in early 2026. Full-year 2025
revenue was approximately $1–2M — almost entirely NRE and small-volume non-automotive
orders. The adjusted EBITDA loss for 2025 was approximately $29–35M. For 2026, Arbe
guides an EBITDA loss of ($28M)–($31M), reflecting cost reductions (15% expense
decrease) and broadened revenue from defense, robotaxi, and marine markets.

**Key signal:** The gap between technological capability (2,304-channel 4D radar) and
commercial revenue ($1–2M) is the defining tension. The Chinese OEM serial production
win via Hirain/LRR610 is the first real production revenue signal, but it is in China,
not Western automotive. Western OEM wins remain 2028–2030.

Mobileye (Nasdaq: MBLY)
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

Mobileye is the only company in this field generating at-scale automotive revenue.
Full-year 2025 revenue reached $1,894M (+15% YoY), with $602M in operating cash flow
(+51% vs 2024). The company ended 2025 with $1.8B in cash. Its 8-year forward revenue
pipeline stands at $24.5B. For 2026, Mobileye guides flat to 5% revenue growth at
approximately 37M+ EyeQ units. The $612M acquisition of Mentee Robotics (humanoid
robotics) extends the company's Physical AI ambitions beyond automotive.

**Key signal:** Mobileye is not a startup — it is a scaled automotive technology
business with a decade of deployment. Its risks are execution on the robotaxi/L3
transition and camera-first weather limitations, not survival.

Innoviz Technologies (Nasdaq: INVZ)
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

Innoviz achieved $55M in 2025 revenue — more than double 2024 — driven by NRE payments
and production shipments from BMW and VW programs. The company secured approximately
$95M in NRE commitments (payments 2025–2027) and raised $40M in a February 2025
registered direct offering. Cash burn fell to approximately $7.3M in Q2 2025. Market
cap as of May 2026 is approximately $145M (stock at $0.67), creating a significant
discount to NRE pipeline value — a notable signal for investors.

**Key signal:** Innoviz has the best combination of production momentum (BMW L3 live,
VW SOP 2026–2027) and next-generation product (InnovizThree, ULR). The sub-$150M
market cap vs $95M+ NRE pipeline suggests the market is not fully pricing in execution.

Luminar Technologies (Nasdaq: LAZR — suspended)
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

Luminar filed Chapter 11 bankruptcy in December 2025 following: the Volvo contract
termination (November 2025), an SEC investigation, forbearance agreements with
noteholders, and suspended FY 2025 guidance. Q3 2025 revenue was $18.7M (+21% YoY)
but insufficient to service debt. Total funding raised was $306M. MicroVision agreed
to acquire Luminar's LiDAR assets in January 2026, targeting preservation of the Halo
IP and OEM development contracts. The Halo ASIC tape-out was completed Q4 2025 and
a low-volume prototype line launched Q1 2026 — meaning the technology is real, the
company is not.

**Key signal:** Luminar is a cautionary tale — strong technology, weak balance sheet,
single-customer concentration (Volvo). The assets (Halo IP, Mercedes relationship,
production know-how) may survive under MicroVision. The brand and independent roadmap
will not.

Vayyar (Private)
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

Vayyar has raised $296M across five rounds, most recently a $108M Series E in June 2022,
reaching a $1B valuation. Investors include Koch Disruptive Technologies, Battery
Ventures, Bessemer Venture Partners, Celesta Capital, and MoreVC. The company has
515 employees and operates across automotive, healthcare, retail, and smart building
verticals. No new funding has been announced since 2022, suggesting either organic
growth sufficiency or quiet fundraising.

**Key signal:** $296M raised with no public 2023–2026 funding round suggests Vayyar
is either cash-flow positive from its multi-vertical model or planning a late-stage
round / IPO. The 4D radar market it addresses is projected at $1.2B by 2030.

Cepton (Koito subsidiary)
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

Cepton was fully acquired by Koito Manufacturing in January 2025. Prior to acquisition,
Cepton had raised $226M including a China Fortune-Tech Capital round. Under Koito
ownership, Cepton is no longer an independent entity — its financials are consolidated
into Koito's, and its IP (37 patents) is Koito's. The strategic value is the GM Ultra
Cruise supply chain, which Koito now controls through to volume production.

**Key signal:** Koito acquisition de-risks Cepton for OEMs — Tier-1 supply chain,
automotive manufacturing quality, and balance sheet stability. But it also removes
Cepton from the independent technology innovation cycle.

IP Landscape Summary
--------------------

The patent landscape in ADAS sensing is heavily segmented by technology category.
Mobileye dominates computer vision and sensor fusion IP. LiDAR IP is fragmented across
Innoviz, Cepton (now Koito), and former-Luminar assets. Radar IP is concentrated at
Arbe and Vayyar. Teradar occupies a greenfield THz position with no credible incumbent.

**THz IP is wide open.** No major automotive OEM, Tier-1, or sensing company holds
meaningful THz automotive patents. Teradar's $175M in funding is primarily protecting
and building this IP moat. The window to establish THz IP dominance is approximately
2025–2028 — before the technology reaches production and standards bodies begin
mandating interoperability.

**LiDAR IP risk from Luminar bankruptcy.** Luminar's 1550 nm and Halo IP entering
bankruptcy proceedings creates uncertainty. MicroVision, if it completes the acquisition,
will control assets that were previously blocking IP for behind-windshield LiDAR
integration. OEMs and Tier-1s should monitor the asset sale outcome closely.

**Mobileye's RSS patent** (Responsibility Sensitive Safety) is a strategic moat
beyond sensors — it defines a mathematical model for assigning fault in AV incidents.
If RSS becomes an industry standard (actively pursued by Mobileye through the ISO
standards process), it could lock OEMs into Mobileye's broader software stack even
if they source sensors elsewhere.
